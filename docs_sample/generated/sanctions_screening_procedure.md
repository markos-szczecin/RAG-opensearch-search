# Sanctions Screening Procedure

## Overview

NovaBanque is committed to maintaining the highest standards of sanctions compliance in accordance with international regulations and best practices. This procedure establishes the mandatory screening protocols for all customers, transactions, and beneficial owners to prevent potential exposure to sanctioned entities, jurisdictions, and individuals.

## Scope and Applicability

This procedure applies to:

- **All new customer onboarding** across retail, corporate, and institutional segments
- **Existing customer transactions** exceeding defined monetary thresholds
- **Wire transfers** and cross-border payment instructions
- **Beneficial ownership verification** during Know-Your-Customer (KYC) processes
- **Third-party service providers** and correspondent banking relationships

All NovaBanque employees, contractors, and agents must comply with these requirements without exception.

## Screening Database and Tools

NovaBanque utilizes an integrated sanctions screening platform that cross-references multiple authoritative sources, including:

- **Global Sanctions Lists**: Comprehensive databases maintained by international regulatory bodies
- **OFAC Designations**: Office of Foreign Assets Control lists and their international equivalents
- **UN Security Council Lists**: Current and historical sanctions designations
- **EU Consolidated List**: European Union sanctions determinations
- **Internal Watch Lists**: NovaBanque-maintained lists of elevated-risk entities and jurisdictions

The screening system performs **real-time fuzzy matching** with name variations, aliases, and transliteration alternatives to capture potential matches with reasonable accuracy.

## Screening Procedures

### Initial Screening

Upon customer onboarding, compliance personnel must:

1. Extract and standardize all identifying information from customer documentation
2. Conduct searches using primary identifiers (full legal name, date of birth, national ID)
3. Review match results with a **positive match threshold** of 75% or higher for manual review
4. Escalate any potential matches to the Sanctions Screening Committee within **24 business hours**

### Transaction Screening

All transactions exceeding €50,000 or equivalent USD value must be screened against current sanctions lists prior to execution. Lower-value transactions from customers with historical elevated risk profiles require screening at the discretion of the transaction officer.

### Ongoing Monitoring

NovaBanque maintains continuous monitoring of existing customer relationships. Screening occurs:

- **Daily**: Against newly updated sanctions lists for active accounts
- **Quarterly**: Comprehensive re-screening of all customer files
- **Event-based**: Following significant customer information updates or sanctions list amendments

## Match Resolution and Escalation

When a potential match is identified:

- **Low-confidence matches** (75–85%) are documented and reviewed by senior compliance staff
- **High-confidence matches** (85%+) are immediately escalated to the Chief Compliance Officer
- **Confirmed matches** result in account suspension and reporting to relevant authorities
- All match documentation is retained for a minimum of seven years

## Training and Accountability

All staff involved in customer-facing or transaction-processing roles must complete **mandatory sanctions compliance training** annually. Departmental heads are responsible for ensuring their teams maintain current certifications.

Non-compliance with this procedure may result in disciplinary action, termination of employment, and regulatory reporting obligations.
