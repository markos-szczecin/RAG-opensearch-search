# Chargeback Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes NovaBanque's framework for managing chargebacks, disputes, and reversal requests initiated by cardholders. The policy ensures fair treatment of all parties while maintaining compliance with payment network regulations and protecting customer funds.

## Scope

This policy applies to all NovaBanque customers, merchants, payment processors, and internal teams involved in dispute resolution. It covers all transaction types processed through NovaBanque's digital payment platform, including debit card transactions, peer-to-peer transfers, and merchant payments.

## Key Definitions

**Chargeback**: A transaction reversal initiated by a cardholder's bank when a customer disputes a charge or alleges unauthorized use.

**Dispute**: A formal complaint filed by a customer regarding a transaction, including claims of fraud, non-delivery, or service failure.

**Representment**: NovaBanque's process of challenging a chargeback by providing evidence supporting the original transaction.

## Chargeback Process

### Customer-Initiated Disputes

Customers may initiate a dispute within **180 days** of the transaction date through the NovaBanque mobile app, web portal, or by contacting our Customer Support team. All disputes must include:

- Transaction date and amount
- Merchant name and reference number
- Detailed description of the complaint
- Supporting documentation (if applicable)

### Investigation Phase

Upon receiving a dispute, NovaBanque's Disputes Team will:

1. Verify transaction authenticity and authorization status
2. Contact the merchant for transaction records and proof of delivery
3. Review communication history between customer and merchant
4. Evaluate chargeback reason codes provided by payment networks

The investigation typically concludes within **30 business days**. Customers will receive status updates throughout the process.

### Resolution Outcomes

- **In Customer Favor**: Transaction amount is refunded to the customer's account within 5 business days
- **In Merchant Favor**: Chargeback is dismissed; merchant retains transaction proceeds
- **Partial Resolution**: Partial refund issued when partial service delivery is confirmed

## Fraud Protection

**Unauthorized Transaction Claims**: NovaBanque investigates all fraud allegations according to payment network standards. Customers reporting unauthorized transactions should provide:

- Dates when they discovered fraudulent activity
- Identification of potentially compromised accounts
- Confirmation they did not authorize the transactions

Customers are not liable for confirmed unauthorized transactions processed without proper authentication.

## Merchant Responsibilities

Merchants accepting NovaBanque payments must:

- Retain transaction receipts and proof of delivery for minimum **90 days**
- Respond to chargeback inquiries within payment network deadlines
- Maintain clear refund and cancellation policies
- Implement appropriate security measures (PCI compliance)

Merchants with excessive chargeback ratios exceeding **1% of total transaction volume** may face increased monitoring, reserves, or account restrictions.

## Representment Rights

NovaBanque and merchants retain the right to challenge chargebacks by submitting compelling evidence, including:

- Signed transaction receipts
- Delivery confirmation and tracking information
- Customer communication records
- IP address and device information matching account history

## Appeals and Escalation

Customers dissatisfied with dispute outcomes may file a formal appeal within **30 days** of the resolution notice. Appeals are reviewed by an independent team member not involved in the initial investigation.

## Policy Compliance

All NovaBanque employees, contractors, and merchants must comply with this policy. Violations may result in account suspension, termination, or regulatory reporting.

For questions or clarifications, contact the Compliance Department at compliance@novabanque.local.
